1.5 Years to 10 Years

Symptoms and Diagnosis

  • Monitoring Growth: Regularly check for nutritional deficiencies due to the restricted diet and consult a nutritionist for guidance.
  • Oral Immunotherapy: In some cases, oral immunotherapy might be an option. Consult with an allergist for more information.

Diet and Nutrition:

  • Nutrient-Rich Alternatives: Ensure that the diet includes a variety of non-legume sources of fiber and protein to maintain balanced nutrition.
  • Cooking Tips: Use non-legume flour alternatives for baking and cooking to avoid accidental exposure.

Social and School Considerations:

  • Allergy Action Plan: Develop an allergy action plan with the school, including steps for accidental exposure and necessary emergency contacts.
  • Birthday Parties and Events: Prepare snacks or meal options for your child to bring to social events to avoid relying on others' food choices.

Organizing Content on the Website

1. Age-Specific Sections:

  • Interactive Guides: Create interactive guides for each age group with checklists and tips specific to managing legume allergies at different developmental stages.

2. Visual Aids:

  • Detailed Infographics: Include infographics that explain symptoms, safe foods, and avoidance strategies for legume allergies.
