ALLERGY TO WHEAT

Wheat Allergy (Celiac Disease) in Children: A Detailed Guide

For Children Aged 6 Months to 1.5 Years

Symptoms and Diagnosis:

1. Initial Symptoms:

  • When wheat-based foods are first introduced, symptoms such as poor growth, bloating, diarrhea, and irritability may develop. These are early indicators of a possible wheat allergy or celiac disease.

2. Diagnosis:

  • Diagnosis is typically confirmed through blood tests that check for specific antibodies associated with celiac disease. In some cases, an intestinal biopsy may be necessary to observe damage to the small intestine caused by gluten.

Gluten-Free Diet:

First Foods:

  • Gluten-Free Cereals: Start with cereals that are naturally gluten-free, such as rice or corn. These are safe for babies with a wheat allergy.
  • Pureed Foods: Pureed fruits (like apples and bananas), vegetables (like sweet potatoes and carrots), and proteins (like lentils and peas) are safe and nutritious options.

Monitoring:

  • Introduce new foods one at a time and wait a few days between each to monitor for any adverse reactions, such as digestive discomfort or skin rashes.
