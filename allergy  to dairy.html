Allergy to Dairy

  • For children with dairy allergies, ensure a balanced diet with dairy-free alternatives
    like plant-based milks and calcium-rich foods. Introduce safe solid foods gradually,
    monitoring for reactions. Use fortified products to meet nutritional needs,
    and be vigilant about cross-contamination. Educate caregivers and school staff,
    and have an emergency plan in place for accidental exposure.

  • Read Labels: Always check food labels for
    hidden dairy ingredients like casein or whey.
  • Allergy Testing: Regularly consult with
    an allergist to monitor the allergy.
  • Calcium Sources: Ensure adequate calcium intake
    through fortified plant-based milks or supplements.
  • Vitamin D: Include vitamin D-rich foods or supplements, as dairy is a primary source.
  • Safe Snacks: Stock up on dairy-free snacks like fruit, vegetables, and dairy-free crackers.
  • Cross-Contamination: Avoid using the same utensils and surfaces for dairy and dairy-free foods.
