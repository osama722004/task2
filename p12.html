• Balanced Meals: Ensure that meals include a variety of fruits, vegetables, proteins (like chicken, fish, and eggs), and gluten-free grains (like quinoa, millet, and buckwheat).

3.Social and School Considerations:

  • Avoid cross-contamination in the kitchen by using separate toasters, cutting boards, and utensils for gluten-free foods. Ensure that surfaces are cleaned thoroughly before preparing gluten-free meals.
For Children Aged 1.5 Years to 10 Years

I. Symptoms and Diagnosis:

Symptoms:
  • As the child grows, symptoms may include fatigue, stomach pain, delayed growth, and behavioral changes. Some children may experience mood swings or irritability if they accidentally consume gluten.
Diagnosis:
  • Regular follow-ups with an allergist are crucial to monitor the child's adherence to a gluten-free diet and ensure that the diet is effectively managing the condition.

2. Diet Expansion:

Gluten-Free Alternatives:
  • Bread and Pasta: Use gluten-free bread and pasta made from rice, corn, or quinoa. These alternatives are widely available and can be incorporated into the child's meals.
  • Snacks: Gluten-free snacks such as rice cakes, gluten-free crackers, and fruit snacks are good options.
